Tribute to Malala Yousafzai(2015)

Biography

Malala Yousafzai was born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Swat Valley, Pakistan. She became an advocate for girls' education at a young age, blogging for the BBC Urdu under a pseudonym about her life during the Pakistani Taliban occupation of Swat.

In 2012, Malala was shot in the head by a Taliban gunman for her activism. She survived and continued her advocacy, co-authoring the memoir 'I Am Malala' and establishing the Malala Fund to champion education for girls globally.

Achievements

  • Youngest-ever Nobel Peace Prize laureate (2014)
  • Co-authored the memoir 'I Am Malala'
  • Established the Malala Fund to advocate for girls' education
  • Delivered a historic speech at the United Nations on her 16th birthday
